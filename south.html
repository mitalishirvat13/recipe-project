Masala Dosa

Thin and crispy South Indian rice crepe stuffed with spiced potato filling, served with chutney and sambar.

Ingredients:

  • Dosa batter - 2 cups (fermented)
  • Potatoes - 3 (boiled & mashed)
  • Onion - 1 large (sliced)
  • Green chilies - 2 (chopped)
  • Ginger - 1 tsp (grated)
  • Curry leaves - few
  • Turmeric powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Mustard seeds - 1/2 tsp
  • Oil - 2 tbsp
  • Salt - to taste

Steps:

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, onion, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté well.
  2. Add turmeric and mashed potatoes. Mix with salt. Potato masala is ready.
  3. Heat a tawa, spread a ladle of dosa batter thinly, drizzle little oil.
  4. Place some potato masala in the center, fold the dosa.
  5. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Idli

Soft and fluffy South Indian steamed rice cakes, usually served with chutney and sambar.

Ingredients:

  • Idli rice - 2 cups
  • Urad dal (split black gram) - 1 cup
  • Fenugreek seeds - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water - as needed

Steps:

  1. Wash and soak rice & dal separately for 4–6 hours (with fenugreek seeds in dal).
  2. Grind rice and dal separately into smooth batter, then mix together.
  3. Add salt, cover, and let it ferment overnight (8–10 hours).
  4. Grease idli molds, pour batter, and steam for 10–12 minutes.
  5. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Medu Vada

Crispy South Indian lentil fritters, soft inside and golden outside, usually served with chutney and sambar.

Ingredients:

  • Urad dal (split black gram) - 1 cup (soaked 4-5 hrs)
  • Green chili - 2 (chopped)
  • Ginger - 1 inch (chopped)
  • Curry leaves - few
  • Black pepper - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil - for deep frying

Steps:

  1. Soak urad dal for 4–5 hours and grind into a smooth thick batter.
  2. Add chopped chili, ginger, curry leaves, pepper, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Wet your hands, take small batter portions, shape like a donut (with a hole in center).
  4. Heat oil and deep fry vadas on medium flame until golden and crispy.
  5. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
