Methi Paratha

Healthy and tasty Indian flatbread made with wheat flour and fresh fenugreek leaves.

Ingredients:

  • Wheat flour - 2 cups
  • Fresh methi (fenugreek) leaves - 1 cup (chopped)
  • Green chili - 1 (finely chopped)
  • Ginger - 1 tsp (grated)
  • Carom seeds (ajwain) - 1/2 tsp
  • Turmeric powder - 1/4 tsp
  • Red chili powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil or ghee - for cooking
  • Water - as needed (for kneading)

Steps:

  1. Mix wheat flour, methi leaves, green chili, ginger, spices, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Add water gradually and knead into a soft dough. Rest for 15 minutes.
  3. Divide dough into small balls and roll each into a flat paratha.
  4. Cook on a hot tawa, applying oil or ghee on both sides until golden brown.
  5. Serve hot with curd, pickle, or chutney.

Aloo Paratha

Delicious Indian flatbread stuffed with spiced potato filling, served with curd or pickle.

Ingredients:

  • Wheat flour - 2 cups
  • Potatoes - 3 medium (boiled & mashed)
  • Onion - 1 small (finely chopped, optional)
  • Green chili - 1 (finely chopped)
  • Coriander leaves - 2 tbsp (chopped)
  • Red chili powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Garam masala - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil or ghee - for cooking
  • Water - as needed (for dough)

Steps:

  1. Knead wheat flour with water and a pinch of salt into soft dough. Rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Mix boiled potatoes with onion, green chili, coriander, and spices to make filling.
  3. Divide dough into balls, roll out slightly, place filling in the center, and seal edges.
  4. Roll out gently into a paratha shape.
  5. Cook on a hot tawa with oil or ghee until golden brown on both sides.
  6. Serve hot with curd, butter, or pickle.

Amritsari Chole Paratha

A flavorful Punjabi paratha stuffed with spicy chole (chickpea) filling, served with curd or pickle.

Ingredients:

  • Wheat flour - 2 cups
  • Boiled chickpeas (chole) - 1 cup
  • Onion - 1 small (finely chopped)
  • Green chili - 1 (finely chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1 tsp
  • Coriander leaves - 2 tbsp (chopped)
  • Chole masala / Garam masala - 1 tsp
  • Red chili powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil or ghee - for cooking
  • Water - as needed (for dough)

Steps:

  1. Knead wheat flour with water and a pinch of salt into soft dough. Rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Mash the boiled chole and mix with onion, chili, ginger-garlic paste, coriander, and spices.
  3. Divide dough into balls, roll out slightly, stuff with chole mixture, and seal edges.
  4. Roll out gently into a paratha shape.
  5. Cook on a hot tawa with oil or ghee until golden brown on both sides.
  6. Serve hot with curd, pickle, or extra chole curry.
